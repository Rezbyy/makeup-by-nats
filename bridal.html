FAQs

Questions brides
always ask

Wedding dates book up quickly, especially during peak season (October–April in New Zealand). Nats recommends enquiring as soon as you have your date confirmed, ideally 9–12 months in advance. If your date is sooner, don't hesitate to reach out as last-minute availability does sometimes open up.

A bridal trial is highly recommended for all brides, and essential for cultural brides or anyone planning two looks. Your trial is a relaxed, pressure-free session to refine your look, test long-wear performance, and ensure your wedding morning runs smoothly. Trials are scheduled 4–8 weeks before the wedding.

Yes! Nats travels to your location: whether that's your home, a hotel, or your venue. Travel fees may apply depending on the distance. Please include your venue location in your enquiry so we can provide accurate information.

The bride's makeup typically takes 60–90 minutes. Bridesmaids and family members are usually 45–60 minutes each. Nats will create a detailed timeline for your morning based on your ceremony start time and the number of people in your party.

Absolutely. With her Sri Lankan heritage and years of experience working across diverse cultural celebrations, Nats has a deep respect and understanding of cultural bridal aesthetics. She can create traditional looks, contemporary looks, or a beautiful blend of both. She also accommodates two looks for brides with multiple ceremonies.

Nats uses a curated selection of high-end, professional-grade products chosen for their longevity, performance in photographs, and suitability across a range of skin tones and types. If you have any skin sensitivities or allergies, please let her know during your consultation so she can select the most suitable products for you.

Yes! Nats works with a team including a Senior MUA and Assistant MUA, making her fully equipped for larger bridal parties. Please include the number of people requiring makeup in your enquiry so she can plan the team and timeline accordingly.
